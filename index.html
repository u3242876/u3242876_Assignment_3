ROBIN BOYD

Robin Boyd (1919–1971) was one of Australia’s most influential architects and design thinkers. Known for his modernist approach, Boyd was a passionate advocate for innovative, functional design. His work critiquing Australian suburbia, particularly in The Australian Ugliness, still resonates today. Boyd's connection to Canberra includes his vision for modernist architecture, which helped shape the city’s unique design landscape. There are a number of residential buildings designed by Robyn Boyd in Canberra, including Clark House and Fenner House, and a few fantastic commercial buildings, most notably: Churchill House.
