ROBIN BOYD

CHURCHILL HOUSE

Churchill House, designed by Robin Boyd in 1969-70 and completed in 1971 for the Winston Churchill Memorial Trust of Australia, exemplifies Late Twentieth-Century Brutalist architecture, characterized by blank walls and off-form concrete. The complex includes an L-shaped main building, a north pavilion wing, a courtyard with a pool, and a two-level carpark. The main building features varied fa√ßades, combining vertical precast panels with narrow windows facing the pool, and horizontal glazing with timber-textured concrete on the south and east elevations. Interiors mix off-form concrete, lightweight partitions, and varied ceiling treatments, though many finishes have been updated.

Churchill House displays several key features of the Late Twentieth Century Brutalist style:
Large areas of blank wall, especially the west wall of the south wing.
Off-form concrete, such as the west wall of the south wing and the spandrels to the south and east wings.
Sloping structural fins, being the structural steel members supporting the glazing at the corner between the east and south wings.
Precast concrete non-loadbearing wall panels, being the cladding to the wings facing the pool area.
Precast fins for sun protection, as above.Vertical slit windows, also being a part of the cladding to the wings facing the pool area.
